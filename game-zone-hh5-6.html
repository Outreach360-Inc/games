HAND IN HAND 5-6

Hand in Hand Flashcards & Games

These games are designed to go along with the Hand in Hand curriculum! Choose from Flashcards, Memory Match, Connect 4, Word Scramble, Word Search, Bingo review, and Board Game review. Perfect for reinforcing the key language from each level.

Outreach360 Word Board

A versatile card workspace. Select specific cards or random ones to practice vocabulary and grammar, run sorting activities, play games, and spark story building. The built-in library covers most vocabulary from Hand in Hand 1–6 and Oxford Discover 1–2, plus selections from Oxford Discover 3 and even more! While it does not align to BrainPOP, you can copy and paste the images from BrainPOP’s Word Play activity and add custom text. Great for flexible curriculum alignment with endless possibilities!

Word Board Bingo

A Bingo game you can play with random cards from the Outreach360 Word Board. Students fill in each space's text box with a full sentence using the relevant word that appeared. Bonus: Challenge students to conjugate verbs in different tenses, write questions, or use comparatives, adverbs of frequency, modals, etc. Each time the page loads, it generates a unique, randomized board so students get their own version. Great for vocabulary and grammar review.

Rhyme Dominoes

Use the Outreach360 Word Board. Deal two random word cards in the center to start two open ends. On each turn, place a card from the palette or a custom card next to a word it rhymes with, then place a random card next to it (since dominoes have 2 sides, but our cards only have 1 word). Play continues until no rhymes can be made or there is no space left on the board (see example board). Add optional scoring (e.g. +1 for using the word in a sentence). Great for phonemic awareness, rhyme families, and vocabulary.

Picture Party 1

Spin for a picture and use one of the suggested activities (located below the notes box) to practice vocabulary, grammar, speaking, writing, and creativity!

Picture Party 2

Spin for a picture with a built-in prompt—or create your own! Students use the image as inspiration for speaking or writing practice. Great for building vocabulary, descriptive language, grammar structures, and creativity.

Mystery Picture

Spin for a mystery picture, then move your cursor over the black area to spotlight parts of it. Students describe what they notice and guess what the picture is (e.g., “It looks metallic — maybe it’s a coin”). Great for warm-ups!

Minimal Pairs Tic-Tac Toe

Students play on a 3×3 grid filled with random target pairs (e.g., ship/sheep). To claim a square, the player must pronounce both words correctly and clearly. First player/team to three-in-a-row wins. Great for boosting pronunciation.

Minimal Pairs Spin & Vote

A fast whole-group listening game. Spin a target pair (e.g., 1=ship, 2=sheep) and choose one of the words to say aloud. Students hold up a 1 or a 2 to vote on which word was said. Reveal the answer, have a few students model it, and give correction as needed. Variation: Say two words (or the same word twice) and have students vote Same or Different in the chat. Great for sound discrimination and pronunciation.

Minimal Pairs Volley

A fast pair game. Partner A “serves” one word from a target pair (e.g., ship); Partner B must instantly “volley” the matching counterpart (sheep). Wrong, late, or repeated = point to the other player. Rotate partners after a few rounds. Great for sound discrimination and pronunciation.

The Tongue Twister Game

Choose a tongue twister and roll a virtual die. Assign each number a style (e.g., 1=whisper, 2=robot, 3=angry, 4=happy, 5=one breath, 6=double speed). Call a few students per roll. Great for pronunciation.

Story Cubes 1

Roll the virtual dice to reveal words, then create a story using all of them! Students can tell the story solo or as a group, adding on one sentence at a time. A fun way to spark imagination, storytelling, and vocabulary use!

Emoji Story

Spin for an emoji sequence. Students tell a story that matches individually or as a group. Great for speaking, writing, and creativity.

GIF Story

Find at least one appropriate GIF online prior to class. Students invent who, when, where, what, why and tell a short story out loud. Great for quick, creative, speaking practice!

Liar

A fast, funny speaking game where each student tells a 30–45 second “true” story with exactly one believable lie hidden inside. The class listens, asks up to two quick questions, then votes true or false. If the liar convinces over half the class, they score; if the class calls it, the class scores. Perfect for practicing persuasive language, tone, and critical listening.

Two Truths and a Lie 1

Present 3 simple sentences—2 true, 1 false. Students guess the lie!

  • Use sentences about familiar topics (e.g., animals, food, daily routines)
  • Or play the classic get-to-know-you version with personal facts.

A fun, flexible way to build confidence and language skills!

Would You Rather? 1

Use the spinner for a fun and flexible speaking or writing activity where students choose between two options and explain their reasoning.Great for practicing opinions, reasoning, and discussion skills. Best suited for Hand in Hand 3 or higher, but easily adaptable for lower levels with visual support.

Whacky Role Plays

Spin for silly scene ideas—use as many as you like! Students act out short, fun role plays based on the prompts. Encourage creativity, exaggeration, and improv. Great for speaking fluency, teamwork, and lots of laughs!

Yes-And Improv

Two students act a short scene about your unit theme. After the first line, every line starts with “Yes, and…” to keep the story going. When you say “Switch!” change which students are acting, or change one thing about the scene—emotion, place, genre, goal, or a speaking rule (e.g., only questions, specific tense). Great for fast, fun speaking practice!

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

Commercial Break

Spin for an item and a tone. Students pretend they’re in a commercial and must sell the item using that tone. Encourage over-the-top expressions, gestures, and creativity. A fun way to build confidence and speaking skills!

Gibberish Translator

One student speaks in expressive gibberish inspired by the prompt—using gestures, tone, sound effects, and facial expressions. Another student “translates” based on those cues. You can use the spinner for ideas or go freestyle. Great for creativity, speaking, and nonverbal communication!

Talk Show Time

Students take turns as hosts and guests. Spin the Guest Wheel to choose a fun character, then spin the Question Wheel throughout the interview to guide the host. Encourage big personalities, curiosity, and creativity. A great way to practice conversation skills and improvisation!

Mystery Guest

One student pretends to be a celebrity, historical figure, or other known person. Others ask open-ended or closed-ended questions to guess who they are. Great for practicing questions and more!

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

Guess Who / Guess Which

Choose from the boards in Kami or use the Canva template to create a custom board with student photos or images related to your unit. The teacher picks a mystery character or item, and students ask yes/no questions to figure it out. Cross off the options they’ve eliminated. Great for practicing questions, descriptions, and vocabulary!

Twenty Questions 2

A classic guessing game that builds critical thinking, questioning skills, and vocabulary! Spin to give a student a mystery item or action. The others ask them yes/no questions to figure out what is—within 20 questions. Great for practicing questions, descriptions, and vocabulary!

Glug

A funny yes/no game: one student secretly chooses a verb, and everyone else tries to guess it by asking questions with the invented verb “glug” (e.g., “Have you ever glugged?”, “Are you glugging now?”). Great for question practice with multiple tenses.

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

Rock, Paper, Scissors – Question Edition

Select 2 students. Have them throw rock-paper-scissors to decide roles. The winner asks one correct question using your target language (e.g., past simple, WH-questions). The other student answers in a full sentence. Each correct question/answer earns a point. Play several rapid rounds, then rotate partners. Great for question formation.

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

What's the Question?

Fill in the board prior to class using statements appropriate for your students’ level (see example here). Divide the class into teams. Pick a square (e.g., “Where? 300”) and read the sentence twice. The first student to raise their hand and form the matching question wins the square and points for their team. Cross off used squares and keep choosing new squares until they’re gone—the highest total wins. If your students are shy, call on students instead of relying on hand raising.

Word Association

One student says any word, and the next quickly says a word that’s related. Keep going through all the students without repeating or hesitating. Great for vocabulary building, quick thinking, and making language connections!

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

Minimum

A fast word-chain game. Put students in small teams, write a starter word on the board (e.g., “table”), and have teams take turns calling out words that begin with the last letter and meet or beat a rising minimum length each round (e.g. 5 letters). Great for vocabulary recall.

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

Alphabet Challenge 1

Pick a topic (spin for one, use your unit theme, or make one up). Students take turns naming words or phrases related to that topic in A–Z order. Great for vocabulary recall, quick thinking, and fun language practice!

Category Race 1

Spin for a topic and challenge students to list as many related words as they can in 30 seconds (or ditch the timer if you prefer)! Play individually, in teams, or as a whole class. Great for building fluency, vocabulary recall, and quick thinking.

Think of a Word

Share your screen with the prompts. Write a noun at the top (e.g., “piano”). Everyone works solo to complete all eight; the first finisher calls “done,” you check, and award a point. Play as many rounds as you'd like. Use nouns from your unit or a word generator. Use nouns with at least 4 letters. Great for vocabulary recall.

The Adventure Game

Spin the randomizer for a fun prompt (e.g., “I’m packing my bag”). The first student starts: “I’m packing my bag. I put in a blue pencil.” Each student repeats the full list and adds one item—encourage short, vivid descriptions! Keep going around the group—more than once if it’s small. Great for memory and vocabulary practice!

Odd One Out 1

Spin for set of words. Students must decide which one doesn’t belong—and explain why! While only one answer is indicated, multiple correct answers are acceptable as long as students can support their reasoning. Great for critical thinking, comparison skills, and vocabulary. Show pictures of each item to support lower levels.

The Bragging Game

A quick escalation activity for comparatives/superlatives and creativity. One student says a simple sentence (e.g., “I live in a house.”); the next “brags” with a bigger claim on the same topic (e.g., “I live in a bigger house.”). Keep topping each other until someone stalls, then switch topics. Great for practicing comparatives and superlatives.

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

A Terrible Day

A board game to practice the simple past—both regular and irregular verbs. Each space on the board comes with a randomized prompt. The game is loaded with hundreds of possible prompts, so it can be played more than once. Great for grammar practice and introducing new verbs.

Free Rice: English Grammar

This multiple-choice game builds English skills and supports the UN World Food Programme—each correct answer donates the equivalent of 10 grains of rice. Use the easiest level (the one the link takes you to). Great for general grammar practice with a purpose!

Speculation

Give a target verb you’re covering in class (e.g., like, have) and the model “I think you ___.” Students make a guess about a classmate (e.g., “I think you like chocolate”). The classmate confirms true/false. Correct guesses earn 1 point; first to 5 wins. Variation: use sentence starters to hit specific grammar (past: “I think you ate…”, present perfect: “I think you’ve…”, future: “I think you’ll…”). Great for grammar, speaking, and listening.

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

Around the World

Two students race to answer first (by mic or chat). The winner moves on to face the next classmate, aiming to go all the way around the group. Play with prompts from Free Rice: English Grammar, Odd One Out 1, etc., or your own questions related to your unit language. Great for fun practice and versatility!

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

English Auction

A fast, flexible game: teams start with a budget (e.g. $100) and bid on “lots” like sentences, vocabulary paired with images, or spelling words. Some lots are correct; others have mistakes. The highest bidder must explain why their lot is correct/best—or fix it on the spot if it isn’t. Award points for smart buys and clear explanations; deduct points for bad buys. Use grammar or vocabulary concepts from your current or previous units. Great for lively language practice and reasoning.

Details

Students watch a short video clip and race to produce examples of a target grammar structure from their current or recent units (e.g., past simple/continuous, comparatives, articles, prepositions, quantifiers, relative clauses, or reported speech). They can either spot and jot exact phrases from the clip or write original sentences about what they see using the target form. After playing, award 1 point for a correct phrase, 3–5 for a correct full sentence. Highest total wins. Great for grammar and listening practice.

Sentence Survivor

If a student makes a mistake, they’re “out.” Play continues around the group until only one student remains. Use with content from your unit. Great for reviewing vocabulary, grammar, and speaking under pressure.

Students go in rotation, each spelling a target word correctly. Use any words from the Cambridge Assessment English Wordlists or words from the Hand in Hand Scope & Sequence. If a student misspells the word, they’re “out.” Play continues until only one student remains. Great for reviewing vocabulary, improving spelling, and building confidence under pressure.

Quick Draw!

Ready, set, sketch: the teacher calls a word and everyone has 30 seconds to draw it. Then everyone reveals their work and compares! Use any words from the Cambridge Assessment English Wordlists or words from the Hand in Hand Scope & Sequence. Great for strengthening listening comprehension and rapid vocabulary recall!

Describe & Draw

Spin for a picture, right-click and copy it, then send it privately to one student. That student describes the picture as clearly as possible while everyone else draws. Depending on your rules and student level, the speaker can explain what’s in the picture or give step-by-step drawing directions using shapes and positions. Afterward, everyone shows their drawings and compares them to the original. A fun way to build speaking and listening skills!

Listen & Draw

The teacher describes an object, scene, or character. Students listen carefully and draw what they hear, then everyone shares their drawings. Use it to review vocabulary and concepts from previous lessons. Great for practicing listening skills!

Find It! 2

Spin for a prompt. Students have 1-2 minutes to find the item in their home and show it on camera. They say the word and describe it in detail. If needed, use visual support to help students understand the prompts. Great for practicing vocabulary and descriptions in an active way.

Show & Tell

Students choose an item from home and show it to the group. They describe it in English—(e.g. what it is, why they like it, where it’s from, or a short story about it). Classmates can ask follow-up questions. Great for building speaking confidence, vocabulary, and conversation skills.
