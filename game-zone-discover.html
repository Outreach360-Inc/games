For a twist, assign red cards and have students defend them—even if they don’t fit! Great for vocabulary and persuasive language!

Perspectives 1

A spin-and-go storytelling game that builds empathy and voice. Each spin serves up a situation, the characters involved, and a creative task. Convince Us 1

Spin a prompt; students try to convince the class to adopt the idea. Every speaker argues the same topic, then the class holds a quick vote for “Most Convincing” (optionally “Best Reason”/“Best Delivery”). Alternatively, spin a fresh prompt for each student, have peers rate the speech 1–5 right after, and announce the top scores at the end. Bonus: Run a peer feedback session after playing. Great for persuasive speaking and listening.

English Auction

A fast, flexible game: teams start with a budget (e.g. $100) and bid on “lots” like sentences, word pairs, definitions, or short paragraphs. Some lots are correct; others have mistakes. The highest bidder must explain why their lot is correct/best—or fix it on the spot if it isn’t. Award points for smart buys and clear explanations; deduct points for bad buys. Use grammar or vocabulary concepts from your current or previous units. Great for lively language practice and reasoning.
