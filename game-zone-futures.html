Photo of the Day

Spark high-level speaking and writing practice! Students use a choice board of challenges to earn the most points before reading the photo caption. After revealing the caption, students delve deeper into the photo. Great for critical thinking, grammar, creativity, and more! Includes variations for shorter activities.

Picture Party 2

Spin for a picture with a built-in prompt. Students use the image as inspiration for speaking or writing practice. Great for building vocabulary, descriptive language, grammar structures, and creativity.

Jeopardy – Idiom Edition

Students compete in a game-show style quiz focused on idioms. Depending on the question, they might guess the meaning, make a sentence, continue a chain story, act out a role-play, or describe what happened before or after someone said the idiom. Teams choose questions by point value and race for the highest score. With 1,300+ idioms appearing at random, the game stays fresh and can be played again and again. A lively way to practice vocabulary, creativity, and real-world language use.

Who Wants to Be a Millionaire? – Vocabulary Edition

A quiz game that makes word learning exciting. Play with the Oxford 5000 (the most important high-frequency English words for learners) or the Academic Word List (the most frequent words in academic texts). Students play as one team, but the teacher calls on students in rotation to answer. To keep it fun, each member of the team may use each lifeline—phone a friend or poll the class—once per game for help.

Description Detective 2

Students guess mystery words using clues! While only one answer is indicated, other answers with the same meaning are accepted. Great for building vocabulary and critical thinking!

Phrasal Frenzy

Spin to get a phrasal verb, then use it to create a sentence or question. To take it further, use different tenses, find a picture and make a meme, write a mini-dialogue, act out a short skit, or design a quick social media-style post. If you don’t know the phrasal verb, look it up in the dictionary. A fun, flexible way to build speaking, grammar, and vocabulary confidence while practicing phrasal verbs.

Combo Challenge

Spin for a base word and race (or take turns) to build strong word combos—collocations and phrasal verbs like “make a decision” or “run into trouble”—boosting vocabulary, fluency, and natural speaking!

Free Rice: English

This multiple-choice game builds English skills and supports the UN World Food Programme—each correct answer donates the equivalent of 10 grains of rice. Choose any English category, trying the easiest level first—then working your way up until you find the perfect challenge. Great for test prep with a purpose!

Around the World

Two students race to answer first (by mic or chat). The winner moves on to face the next classmate, aiming to go all the way around the group. Play with prompts from Free Rice: English, Who Wants to Be a Millionaire? – Vocabulary Edition, Description Detective 2, Odd One Out 3, etc., or your own questions related to your unit language. Great for fun practice and versatility!

Sentence Survivor

Students go in rotation, each saying a correct sentence with the target word, grammar, or topic. If a student makes a mistake, they’re “out.” Play continues around the group until only one student remains. Use with content from your unit or a word of the day. Great for reviewing vocabulary, grammar, and speaking under pressure.

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

Spelling Survivor

Students go in rotation, each spelling a target word correctly. For Oxford Discover Futures 1-3, use words from your current or previous level. For other teens, use words from the Academic Word List. If a student misspells the word, they’re “out.” Play continues until only one student remains. Great for reviewing vocabulary, improving spelling, and building confidence under pressure.

Revealed

A daily puzzle game from Britannica. Guess the topic using the fewest Reveals and Hints. Reveal a word by tapping a black box. Every 2 reveals, you get a Hint to use. Tap the eye to reveal the letter above. Guess any time without penalty. Great for reading comprehension, vocabulary recall, critical thinking, and Duolingo English Test practice!

Story Bingo

First, the teacher should choose a story from CommonLit.org for students to read. Use the board afterward as a post-reading reflection (individual or pair work), or as a group challenge or class competition. Each time the page loads, it generates a unique, randomized board so students get their own version. Great for reading comprehension, story elements, and interactive analysis.

The Essay Arena

Spin twice: once for an essay topic and once for a challenge. Each challenge focuses on a key part of the writing process—like thesis statements, hooks, outlines, evidence, or full paragraphs. Students compete for the best response, then vote, discuss, or revise together.

Boost It!

Spin to get a short, vague statement. The challenge is to clarify and expand the idea using more precise language, examples, and academic vocabulary —while correcting errors if needed. Teachers and students can also create their own statements based on unit themes. Great for: developing fluency, coherence, and idea elaboration.

Paraphrase Duel

Two students take turns paraphrasing the same sentence in different ways. The class (or teacher) decides whose version is clearer, more accurate, or more advanced. Use sentences from news articles, encyclopedia entries, essays, or TOEFL-style texts. Great for vocabulary grammar, and mastering paraphrasing!

Summary Relay

Start with a short text. The first student summarizes it in their own words. The next student then summarizes that summary—and so on! The goal is to gradually reduce the content while keeping the main idea intact. Great for practicing paraphrasing, main idea extraction, listening, and fluency!

Beat the Clock — Future Edition

Spin for an action/event, a time phrase, and a future tense (in the past, simple, continuous, perfect, or perfect continuous). Students write as many affirmative, negative, or interrogative sentences as possible before time runs out. They can adjust the action or time phrase to make the sentence natural and add details to keep each one unique. Award points for each correct sentence. For a twist, add extra time for short stories, dialogues, or news reports, or hide a spinner and let students choose one element themselves. Great for building speed, accuracy, and confidence with future tenses.

What's Going On in This Graph?

Each week, The New York Times shares a new graph, map, or chart for students to notice, wonder, and discuss. On Thursdays, it posts additional background information and analysis + a new graph. Teachers can use current or past graphs. Great for describing charts and graphs and preparing for IELTS (English proficiency test).


Gamify it: Award points for clear answers or strong use of vocabulary and grammar. Have students ask and answer questions about the graph, give them 30–60 seconds to describe as much as they can, or challenge them to compete for the best 150-word paragraph.

Critics' Corner

Students become critics of things they already know—like movies, music, apps, games, or food—or of something they’ve just read, watched, or learned in class. They give ratings, share reviews, and explain their opinions, with options like short written reviews, live “critic shows,” or debates between reviewers. A fun way to practice speaking, writing, persuasion, and critical thinking while connecting English to real experiences.


Connect this game to classroom learning with a story, article, or text students are reading—or by choosing material from CommonLit.org (you will need to create a free account).

Focus Group: Teen Edition

Spin for product, service, or business ideas and act as a focus group to react. Play any way you’d like! A versatile game that builds speaking, debating, critical thinking, and collaboration!


Any of the activities can be adapted into writing tasks!

Shark Tank: Teen Edition

Spin for products, services, or projects. Students design and pitch them to a panel of “sharks” (classmates or teacher). Choose how you play! Add twists like speed pitches (only 60 seconds), written pitches, or team roles (designer, marketer, finance, etc. each present part of the pitch). Boost creativity with slogans, jingles, posters, or commercials. Add challenges such as changing the audience (kids, parents, teachers, world leaders) or demanding changes (make it eco-friendly, cheaper, or cooler). A lively way to build speaking, writing, persuasion, creativity, and critical thinking!

Law & Order: Teen Edition

Spin for a scenario, then bring it to life! Try a mini mock trial (act it out as judge, jury, lawyers, witnesses), a sentencing circle (class decides a fair punishment), or a punishment vs. forgiveness debate. Rank crimes from least to most serious, say a punishment and discuss if it's fair, create a news report, run a police investigation with the students' own clues and statements, or make a comic strip/storyboard of the case. Great for speaking, writing, critical thinking, creativity, and teamwork!

Mini United Nations

Students become country delegates and tackle global dilemmas through role-play, debate, or creative problem-solving. Spin for a scenario and choose from options like press conferences, hot seats, drafting resolutions, emergency sessions, or creating media such as news reports or posters. A dynamic way to practice speaking, writing, critical thinking, or collaboration while exploring global issues!

Agree or Disagree? 2

Spin to reveal a statement. Students choose a side and defend it—perfect for speaking, quick writes, or short essays. A fun way to build persuasive language, reasoning, and speaking skills.


You can use the same statements to hold a class debate.

The Last Word

Spin for a topic, then work together to build a shared opinion. Each person adds one short reason or idea without repeating. The group keeps going until no one can think of something new. Great for speaking and critical thinking.

Would You Rather? 3

Use the spinner for a fun and flexible speaking or writing activity where students choose between two options and explain their reasoning. Great for practicing opinions, reasoning, and discussion skills.

What Would You Do?

A thoughtful speaking or writing activity where students respond to situations. Spin for a prompt. Students explain what they would do and why—building fluency, critical thinking, and conditional language skills.

Apples to Apples

Spin for a set of cards. Each student picks a red card they think fits best with the green card. One judge chooses the funniest or most fitting! Use the shared set of cards or give students the link to the game to spin their own set of red cards (and ignore the green one). For a twist, assign red cards and have students defend them—even if they don’t fit! Great for vocabulary and persuasive language!

Let's Talk

Spin for a discussion question to get students speaking! Some questions are personal, others may require quick research, reasoning, or creativity. Great for practicing fluency, idea development, critical thinking, and speaking confidence!

Talk for a Minute / One-Minute Story

Spin for a topic or use one from your unit. Students take turns speaking for one full minute. You can use the same topic for the whole group or spin a new one for each student. Great for speaking fluency, confidence, and organizing ideas under pressure!

Five-Word Story 2

Spin for a topic, then create a complete story using only five words. This fast, creative challenge pushes students to be imaginative and concise, while practicing vocabulary and grammar in a fun way. Great for quick speaking and warm-ups!

Story Cubes 2

Roll the virtual dice to reveal words, then create a story using all of them! Students can tell the story solo, in pairs, or as a group, adding on one sentence at a time. A fun way to spark imagination, storytelling, and vocabulary use!

Genre Lab 2

Students experiment with different fiction genres through creative writing challenges! Spin for a genre and choose your mode: solo, pairs, or chain story (taking turns sentence by sentence). Great for practicing genre-specific style, tone, and structure while building key writing skills—with plenty of room for imagination and fun!

In a Pickle

Can a giraffe fit in a phone booth? Does a sofa fit in a shopping cart? It all depends on how you size it up in this game of creative thinking and outrageous scenarios. Fit smaller things into bigger ones to win. Great for vocabulary, speaking, and practicing comparatives and superlatives.

Kids Create Absurdity

Spin a silly prompt and have students compete to come up with the funniest, most creative response—answers can be longer than one word, depending on the prompt. A judge picks the winner, and the group can build a story around the winning idea. Perfect for laughter, language, and imagination!

Real Life Role Plays 2

Spin for a scenario. Brainstorm ideas or solutions as a group, then act it out. Afterward, reflect on what worked, what didn’t, and what other choices could have been made. A powerful way to build confidence, problem-solving, and real-life communication skills.

Oxford Phrase List Role Plays 2

The Oxford Phrase List is a collection of the most common and useful phrases in English—expressions learners need for real conversations. Spin to get six phrases, then act out a role-play using them naturally. Sb is an abbreviation for somebody. Sth is an abbreviation for something. A fun way to build fluency, confidence, and real-life communication skills.

Whacky Role Plays

Commercial Break

Gibberish Translator

Twenty Questions 2

A classic guessing game that builds critical thinking, questioning skills, and vocabulary! Use a word from the generator and send it privately to one student. The others ask them yes/no questions to figure out what is—within 20 questions. Adjust the level of the words as appropriate. Great for practicing questions and more!

Taboo

Mad Gab

Charades 2

Act it out—no speaking allowed! Use a word from the generator and send it privately to one student. They act it out while others guess. After each round, have students make a sentence using the word. Adjust the level of the words as appropriate. Great for vocabulary practice, body language, and confident communication in a fun, active way!

Pictionary 2

Draw it—no words or letters allowed! Use a word from the generator and send it privately to one student. They draw it while others guess. After each round, have students make a sentence using the word. Adjust the level of the words as appropriate. Great for vocabulary practice, visual thinking, and confident communication in a fun, active way!

Synonym/Antonym Challenge 2

Spin for a word, then race to name either a synonym or an antonym. A playful way to build fast, flexible word knowledge!

English Tennis

Spin for a category or use your unit’s theme, grammar, word study topic, etc. The first student says a related word or sentence, then the second responds with another—like a rally! Keep going back and forth between the two students until someone gets stuck. Great for vocabulary, reinforcing language patterns, and quick thinking!

Word Association

One student says any word, and the next quickly says a word that’s related. Keep going through all the students without repeating or hesitating. Great for vocabulary building, quick thinking, and making language connections!

Boggle

  • ⬜ Gray = letter not in the word

Word Ladder
