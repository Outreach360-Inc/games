HAND IN HAND 1–4

Hand in Hand Flashcards & Games

These games are designed to go along with the Hand in Hand curriculum! Choose from Flashcards, Memory Match, Connect 4, Word Scramble, Word Search, Bingo review, and Board Game review. Perfect for reinforcing the key language from each level.

Outreach360 Word Board

A versatile card workspace. Select specific cards or random ones to practice vocabulary and grammar, run sorting activities, play games, and spark story building. The built-in library covers most vocabulary from Hand in Hand 1–6 and Oxford Discover 1–2, plus selections from Oxford Discover 3 and even more! While it does not align to BrainPOP, you can copy and paste the images from BrainPOP’s Word Play activity and add custom text. Great for flexible curriculum alignment with endless possibilities!

Word Board Bingo

A Bingo game you can play with random cards from the Outreach360 Word Board. Students fill in each space's textbox with a full sentence using the relevant word that appeared. Each time the page loads, it generates a unique, randomized board so students get their own version. Great for vocabulary and grammar review. Best suited for Hand in Hand 3-4, but you can adapt for lower levels by playing as a group.

Teaching Manipulatives

A flexible set of visual tools to support beginner English learners using Hand in Hand Levels 1–4. Reinforce routines, alphabet, numbers, colors, shapes, spelling, writing, grammar, and vocabulary. Activities are grab-and-go—just apply your unit’s language and teach on the fly!

Picture Party 1

Spin for a picture and use one of the suggested activities (located below the notes box) to practice vocabulary, grammar, speaking, writing, and creativity!

Hidden Pictures

Students search for small items hidden in a larger image to practice visual scanning and vocabulary. Repeat the words, describe them, or use them in a sentence.

Spot the Difference

Show two almost-identical images. Students describe what’s changed using full sentences. Great for target language like there is/are, prepositions, etc.

Mystery Picture

Spin for a mystery picture, then move your cursor over the black area to spotlight parts of it. Students describe what they notice and guess what the picture is (e.g., “It looks metallic — maybe it’s a coin”). For lower levels, preview the picture before class and create a word bank (e.g., shiny, round—with matching icons or Spanish translations) and sentence starters. Toggle between Mystery Picture and your language support as needed. Great for warm-ups!

Chain Story

Work together to build a fun and silly story—one sentence at a time! Students take turns adding to the story, listening carefully and using their imagination to keep it going. Great for practicing speaking, writing, sequencing, and collaboration!

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

GIF Story

Find at least one appropriate GIF online prior to class. Students invent who, when, where, what, why and tell a short story out loud. Great for quick, creative, speaking practice!

Odd One Out 1

Spin for set of words. Students must decide which one doesn’t belong—and explain why! While only one answer is indicated, multiple correct answers are acceptable as long as students can support their reasoning. Great for critical thinking, comparison skills, and vocabulary. Show pictures of each item to support lower levels.

Around the World

Two students race to answer first (by mic or chat). The winner moves on to face the next classmate, aiming to go all the way around the group. Play with prompts from Odd One Out 1, an online English game, or your own questions related to your unit language. Great for fun practice and versatility!

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

Beat the Clock

The teacher gives a topic, word, or grammar focus. Students race against a timer to write as many correct sentences as they can before time runs out. Award points for each correct sentence. Great for reviewing vocabulary, grammar, and writing under pressure. Best suited for Hand in Hand 3–4 as lower levels know limited vocabulary and grammar.

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

Spelling Survivor

Students go in rotation, each spelling a target word correctly. Use familiar vocabulary from the Hand in Hand Scope & Sequence to match your students’ level. If a student misspells the word, they’re “out.” Play continues until only one student remains. Great for reviewing vocabulary, improving spelling, and building confidence under pressure.

Sentence Survivor

Students go in rotation, each saying a correct sentence with the target word, grammar, or topic. If a student makes a mistake, they’re “out.” Play continues around the group until only one student remains. Use with content from your unit. Great for reviewing vocabulary, grammar, and speaking under pressure. Best suited for Hand in Hand 3–4 as lower levels know limited vocabulary and grammar.

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

Quick Draw!

Ready, set, sketch: the teacher calls a word and everyone has 30 seconds to draw it. Then everyone reveals their work and compares! Use words your students are familiar with. Use familiar vocabulary from the Hand in Hand Scope & Sequence to match your students’ level. Great for strengthening listening comprehension and rapid vocabulary recall!

Listen & Draw

The teacher describes an object, scene, or character. Students listen carefully and draw what they hear, then everyone shares their drawings. Use it to review vocabulary and concepts from previous lessons, such as shapes, colors, numbers, prepositions of place, appearance, etc. Great for practicing listening skills!

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

Describe & Draw

Spin for a picture, right-click and copy it, then send it privately to one student. That student describes the picture as clearly as possible while everyone else draws. Depending on your rules and student level, the speaker can explain what’s in the picture or give step-by-step drawing directions using shapes and positions. Afterward, everyone shows their drawings and compares them to the original. A fun way to build speaking and listening skills!

Simon Says

A fast, active listening game where students follow commands only if they begin with “Simon says.” If the leader says “Simon says touch your nose,” students do it; if the leader just says “Touch your nose” and someone moves, they’re out. A fun way to practice listening, focus, and quick reactions while reviewing vocabulary for actions, body parts, and more.

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

Find It! 1

Teacher calls out a word or category (something blue, an animal, something you can eat). Students have 30–60 seconds to find the item in their home and show it on camera. They say the word or make a short sentence (“It’s my blue pen”). Use categories with simple vocabulary relevant to your unit. Great for practicing vocabulary and simple sentences in an active way.

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

Show & Tell

Students choose an item from home and show it to the group. They describe it in English—(e.g. what it is, why they like it, where it’s from, or a short story about it). Classmates can ask simple follow-up questions. Adjust the level of detail to your students’ abilities. Great for building speaking confidence, vocabulary, and conversation skills.

THIS GAME DOES NOT REQUIRE ANY MATERIALS

The Q&A Game

Get students speaking or writing! Spin a question. Students practice forming complete sentences. Great for warm-ups, fluency practice, and building confidence. Best suited for Hand in Hand 3–4, but easily adaptable for lower levels.

Would You Rather? 1

Use the spinner for a fun and flexible speaking or writing activity where students choose between two options and explain their reasoning. Great for practicing opinions, reasoning, and discussion skills. Best suited for Hand in Hand 3 or higher, but easily adaptable for lower levels with visual support.

Mystery Sound 1

Play a sound and have students guess what it is! Use the sounds in the presentation or find sounds related to your unit. Great for listening, critical thinking, and introducing new vocabulary!

Charades 1

Act it out—no speaking allowed! Use the Hand in Hand Scope & Sequence to choose a word your students have learned and send it privately to one student. They act it out while others guess. After each round, have students make a sentence using the word. Great for vocabulary review, body language, and confident communication in a fun, active way!

Pictionary 1

Draw it—no words or letters allowed! Use the Hand in Hand Scope & Sequence to choose a word your students have learned and send it privately to one student. They draw it while others guess. After each round, have students make a sentence using the word. Great for vocabulary review, visual thinking, and confident communication in a fun, creative way!

Hangman

A classic spelling and vocabulary game! Use the Hand in Hand Scope & Sequence to choose a word your students have learned and draw blanks for each letter. Students guess letters one at a time—if they’re right, fill them in; if not, draw a part of the hangman. After each round, have students make a sentence using the word. Great for spelling, letter recognition, vocabulary review, and logical thinking.

City Guesser

An online geography trivia game that immerses players in a random video walkthrough of a location. You explore the surroundings to gather visual clues. Using these observations, you guess your location on a map. Great for strengthening geography and problem-solving skills!

GeoGuesser

An online geography trivia game that immerses players in a random location from Google Street View. You explore the surroundings to gather visual clues, identifying languages, road signs, landscapes, or architecture. Using these observations, you guess your location on a world map, scoring points based on accuracy. Great for strengthening geography, spatial awareness, and problem-solving skills!

Globle

Every day, there is a new Mystery Country. Your goal is to guess the mystery country using the fewest number of guesses. Each incorrect guess will appear on the globe with a colour indicating how close it is to the Mystery Country. The hotter the colour, the closer you are to the answer. Great for geography practice and critical thinking!
